JAI SRI KRISHNA{{KANNAYA}}

Lord Krishna is the Hindu god of love, compassion, and protection. Known as the Dark One, he is often depicted with dark blue skin. Krishna is an avatar of the god Vishnu, but he is also worshiped as a god in his own right. Hindu mythology is filled with tales about Krishna's life.

Matsya; Kurma; Varaha; Narasimha; Vamana; Parashurama; Rama; Krishna or Balarama; Buddha or Krishna; and Kalki.

NAMES OF AVATAR MEANING PHOTO
MASTYA AVATAR Matsya is the half fish, a half-human avatar of Vishnu.
KURMA AVATAR Kurma is the second avatar of Vishnu in a half tortoise half-man form.
VARAHA AVATAR Varaha is the half-man and half-boar avatar of Vishnu.
NARASIMHA AVATAR Narasimha is the half-lion and half-human avatar of Lord Vishnu.
VAMANA AVATAR The 5th avatar of Lord Vishnu, Vamana, was a dwarf Brahmin.
PARUSHURAMA AVATAR The Sixth avatar of Lord Vishnu, Parshuram, is depicted as a sage with an axe in his hand.
RAMA AVATAR Lord Rama is one of the most significant and powerful deities of Hindu dharma and the lead character of the Ramayana.
KRISHNA AVATAR Lord Krishna is the eighth avatar of Lord Vishnu.
BUDDHA AVATAR Siddhartha Gautam, later known as Gautam Buddha, is the ninth avatar of Vishnu.
KALKI AVATAR Kalki is the only avatar of Vishnu that is yet to be born.
